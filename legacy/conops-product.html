1 · The capability

What it does — and what that replaces

Today, aerial shark monitoring couples detection to continuous human attention: a pilot flying, an observer watching live video. This system decouples them. The operator tasks an area and supervises; the aircraft searches, recognises, and observes on its own.

SEARCH SMART

The aircraft maintains a live probability map of where a target is most likely and flies toward the highest-value water — no wasted passes over areas it has just cleared, coverage biased to where it matters.

RECOGNISE ONBOARD

A neural detector runs on the aircraft itself. Recognition doesn't depend on a video link, ground computers, or an operator's eyes — and a single false frame never triggers a response: confidence must build and hold before the aircraft acts.

OBSERVE & REPORT

On a confirmed detection the aircraft transitions itself to an observation orbit, logs the sighting with position and time, and relays a report to the ground station the moment the link allows.
