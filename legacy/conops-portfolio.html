1 · Mission need & operational objective

Why this system exists

Coastal shark monitoring today depends on crewed aircraft, tethered balloons, or manually piloted drones with a human watching live video. All three couple detection to a person's continuous attention. The operational gap is persistence with autonomous recognition: an aircraft that searches efficiently, decides onboard when it has found something, and changes its own behaviour to observe and report.

OBJECTIVE 1 — COVER

Search a designated coastal area efficiently, biasing coverage toward where a target is most likely, at energy-optimal loiter speeds.

OBJECTIVE 2 — RECOGNISE

Detect targets with an onboard neural detector; accumulate confidence across frames so a single false frame never drives behaviour.

OBJECTIVE 3 — ACT & REPORT

On a gated detection, transition autonomously to orbit-and-observe; log a geolocated, time-stamped detection and relay a summary to the ground.

The operator's role shifts from flying and watching to tasking and supervising: define the area, launch, monitor health, and act on detection reports. The aircraft is never dependent on the operator — or the radio link — to make the search→track decision.

        4 · Degraded & emergency operations

        Failure-mode explorer

        Design rule: every loss degrades to an autopilot-handled safe state. Select a failure to trace the response chain. Failsafe behaviours verified in SITL — Phase 3

        5 · Communications architecture

        Links — and what does not depend on them

        Three independent links connect aircraft and ground. The defining property of the architecture: the detection→track decision loop is entirely onboard (highlighted) — link loss degrades situational awareness, never autonomy.

        Loss of any link engages the corresponding PX4 failsafe (Section 4). Detection events are logged onboard regardless of link state and relayed when the link permits.

        7 · Data & logging concept

        Everything is reconstructable

        The log set is a deliverable, not a by-product: any incident, decision, or detection must be reconstructable after the fact from onboard records.

        ⚠ PRIVACY
        Coastal imagery can capture people and public spaces. Retention, access, and handling of detection imagery are deliberate design items, separate from aviation regulation.

        8 · Regulatory & operational constraints

        Operating context ⚠ Verify with CASA before any flight

        AVIATION (CASA)

        RPA operation in Australia is governed by CASA; obligations depend on weight, location, and recreational vs commercial/research purpose. Persistent over-water patrol trends toward BVLOS, which carries specific approval requirements. Current category, registration, accreditation/licensing, and area approvals must be confirmed against current CASA guidance — none are asserted here.

        DESIGN-FOR-COMPLIANCE

        The stack is built to make compliant operation easy rather than to enforce regulation: geofence and RTL configured and verified in the autopilot (not assumed), the companion computer architecturally unable to override a failsafe, and logging sufficient to reconstruct any incident.
