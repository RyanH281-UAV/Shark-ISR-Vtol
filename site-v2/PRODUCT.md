# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Engineering recruiters, hiring managers, graduate school admissions committees, and potential collaborators in the autonomous systems / robotics space. They arrive as strangers to the project — they may not know ROS 2, PX4, or VTOL ISR. They leave either convinced Ryan can build serious autonomous systems, or they leave.

## Product Purpose

An engineering portfolio built around the shark-ISR VTOL project. The site does two jobs simultaneously: it explains what was built, and it *is* an example of what Ryan can build. Every interactive element is proof of skill, not decoration. Success looks like: a recruiter or admissions reader finishes the page and forwards it.

The navigation stack (Bayesian search, confidence-gated SEARCH → TRACK transition, onboard link-independent detection) is the main deliverable — the Hornet airframe is the vehicle. Structure the experience around the decision loop, not the drone.

## Brand Personality

Ambitious, technical, capable. Flashy in the engineering sense: bold visuals, interactive demonstrations, 3D drone model, simulated mission. Not flashy in the marketing sense — no hype, no superlatives, no claims beyond what was actually built. The boldness is earned.

This is a personal portfolio — Ryan is a person, not a project page. The voice is an engineer who built something difficult and isn't being coy about it.

## Anti-references

- **AI portfolio template**: cream/sand/warm-neutral backgrounds, eyebrow-on-every-section, numbered-section scaffolding (01 / 02 / 03 as decoration, not sequence)
- **Generic SaaS landing page**: hero-metric grids, gradient cards, "trusted by" badges, feature-tour bullet lists
- **Timid / restrained**: muted grays, underselling the work, design that apologises for itself
- **Drone company marketing site**: DJI/Skydio consumer tone — this is R&D autonomy engineering, not a product for sale

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't list** — interactive elements (3D drone model, mission simulation, autonomy loop) demonstrate capability; prose confirms it, never replaces it.
2. **Navigation stack is the hero** — structure the experience around the guidance decision loop; the airframe is context, not the story.
3. **Dark and unafraid** — warmth lives in the Fraunces serif, the engineering content, and precision motion; not in warm-neutral backgrounds that signal "AI made this."
4. **Bold but earned** — every visually impressive moment must have engineering substance behind it; flair without grounding is decoration, not portfolio.
5. **One engineer, not a product** — voice, footer, and framing should reflect Ryan Hughes specifically; anonymous-project tone is the wrong register for a personal portfolio.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Reduced motion support required (already stubbed in globals.css — maintain it throughout). No special user needs flagged beyond standard.
